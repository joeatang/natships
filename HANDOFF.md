## 6 · The format pivot (CRITICAL — no card frame)

**We are NOT making cards.** We're making **cinematic stills**.

### Why this matters

- **Common viewers don't read chemistry or rocket telemetry.** They feel light, color, depth, awe. The art must hit emotionally BEFORE any label is read.
- **Card-format art = museum specimen = Hi Imprints aesthetic.** Drops the resemblance instantly when we ditch the frame.
- **The viral NFT projects don't crowd their image with data.** Pudgy Penguins, BAYC, Cryptopunks, Art Blocks, Beeple stills — pure image. Data lives in metadata. We follow that pattern.
- **The chemistry/physics still drives every visual element** — it's atmospheric, not labeled. You don't need to know the nebula is a cymatic interference of three frequencies to feel its beauty.

### Format spec

- **Aspect ratio:** square 1500×1500 (NFT marketplace standard) — OR widescreen 1920×1080 for hero variants. Pick square for the main render.
- **Full bleed.** Background-to-edge. No frame. No double-rule border. No engraved scrollwork.
- **No data plate on the image.** No periodic-table cell. No HUD readout panels.
- **Composition is cinematic.** The vessel is staged dramatically — not centered like a specimen. Use rule-of-thirds, atmospheric depth, lens-flare moments at engine exhaust, light scatter through nebula.
- **Aesthetic references:** SpaceX press renders · Apollo era propaganda posters · Stanley Donwood (Radiohead album covers) · Beeple cinematic stills · Soviet space art · Hubble Deep Field plates · Denis Villeneuve film stills (Dune, Arrival, Blade Runner 2049).
- **Tone:** awe + wonder + technical seriousness — not lab report, not museum mount.

### Where the data lives

Three tiers, in order of visibility:

1. **The image itself** (zero text on it, except maybe a 4–6px hash-prefix watermark in one corner like a film stamp). Pure visual.
2. **Optional "Telemetry Mode" overlay** (toggle on the project website / NFT viewer) — shows HUD readouts, fuel chemistry, flight log, mission patch detail. For collectors who want to dig in.
3. **UNAT inscription metadata** — all 30+ traits available to any wallet / marketplace / explorer. Where rarity tools and trait filters operate.

### Where the HI nod lives

- A tiny inscribed signature on the rocket hull itself — looks like a manufacturer's mark on the airframe, two characters, lowercase "hi" (almost invisible at thumbnail size, discoverable at full res). NOT a frame engraving.
- Plus the **"Imprint Studio"** name in the trait metadata under `studio`.

### The invitation layer

The project must feel WELCOMING to non-technical viewers while rewarding technical ones. Tactics:

- **Plain-language story:** Every vessel has a CALL SIGN (e.g. `ANTARES · ANT-1824`) and a FLEET (e.g. `Antares Fleet`). Fleets become tribes — anyone can pick one to root for.
- **Universal visual vocabulary:** Rockets + space = recognizable to a 6-year-old. The chemistry is *embedded* in the beauty, not flaunted as a barrier.
- **One-sentence pitch on the landing page:** *"Ships forged from the chain. Skies forged from the chain. One per block. Built to return."* No jargon.
- **Optional deep-dive page** for collectors who want to learn how it all derives from block data. Curiosity, not gatekeeping.
- **No equations on the art.** No mol-formula strings on the hull. No Δv labels in the foreground.
